The Different Oceans

Pacific Ocean

The Pacific Ocean is the largest and deepest ocean in the world, it covers 60 million square meters and has a depth of 4,000 meters

North Atlantic Ocean

The North Atlantic ocean is a large ocean that covers 11% of the Atlantic Ocean and has a depth of around 3.5 thousand meters.

South Atlantic Ocean

The South Atlantic Ocean is part of the Atlantic Ocean that is south of the equator and makes up the ocean from the east coast of Brazil to the Gulf of Guinea.

Artic Ocean

The Artic Ocean is the smallest and shallowest of the five major oceans around the world covering around 4% of the earth's surface.

Indian Ocean

The Indian Ocean is the second smallest and youngest ocean of the five major oceans spanning around 20% of the earths water surface.

Animals

Animals in the ocean

There are many animals in the ocean ranging from small to large, like on land they have many different sizes and shapes. The oceans are home to about 230,000 known species in only the 5% of the ocean we have explored, this means there could be millions more yet to be explored. All the difference animals have different adaptations to help them live in different temperatures and different depths in the ocean.  

Find Out More

Ocean life is grouped into 7 main groups, Shellfish, which are a group that refer to aquatic invertebrates such as crustaceans, molluscs and echinoderms. Another group is fish which consists of gills but lack limbs and can range from small to larger sizes. The other 5 groups of animals truly show you the variation of animals that live in the ocean.
